    Quick start guide
    1
    Create a site — On the Sites tab, click + New site. Enter the site name, lighting zone (LZ0–LZ3), survey date, your name, and contact email. One site per physical location; you can have multiple survey years per site.
    2
    Add luminaires — Go to the Inventory tab, select your site, and click + Add fixture for each outdoor luminaire. Fill in as many fields as possible. Hover the ? icons for illustrated guidance on any field.
    3
    Take photos — With GPS enabled on your phone camera (see GPS Setup below), photograph each luminaire. Upload via the Photos & Spectra tab. GPS coordinates are extracted automatically from the photo.
    4
    Add spectrum data — If you have a spectrometer app, take a screenshot showing the spectral power distribution and upload it to the fixture's spectrum slot.
    5
    Set compliance — For each fixture, set the Compliant? field based on the RASC 5 principles. A fixture is compliant if it meets Need, Target, Quantity, Colour (≤2700K), and Control criteria.
    6
    Export CSV — Go to Report & Map and click CSV. Save this file — it contains all your data including photos and can be used to restore your work on any device.
    7
    Return next year — Import your saved CSV on the Import tab. In Report & Map, click Start new survey year — all fixtures carry over and you update only what has changed. The report will show year-over-year improvements.
    RASC 5-principle framework
    Field-by-field reference
    Site fields
    Site nameThe name of the property or dark sky site being surveyed. Be consistent year to year.
    Lighting zoneLZ0 = intrinsically dark (wilderness, DSR). LZ1 = low (developed area in DSS). LZ2 = moderate (rural). LZ3 = suburban.
    Survey dateDate the survey was conducted. Used for year-over-year tracking.
    Fixture identity
    Fixture IDYour reference label, e.g. F-01, P-03. Use a system that matches your site map if you have one.
    Location / areaWhere the fixture is — building name, area, or landmark. e.g. "Main entrance", "Parking lot north".
    Purpose / targetWhat the light is for. Choose the closest match from the dropdown. This relates to the RASC "Need" and "Target" principles.
    Activity levelHow intensively the area is used. Low (~22 lux min): parking, paths. High (~54 lux min): main entries. Visual task: work areas requiring specific illumination.
    No. of luminairesHow many identical fixtures at this location. If they differ, enter them as separate fixture records.
    Luminaire details
    Luminaire typeThe fixture housing style — wallpack, bollard, roadway arm, spot, etc. Hover the ? for illustrated examples.
    Shielding stateFully = all light directed downward, no upward spill. Partially = some upward or sideward spill. Unshielded = bare bulb or globe with light in all directions.
    Mounting heightHeight of the luminaire in metres. Estimate if exact value unknown.
    Lamp details
    Lamp typeThe light source technology. LED – Amber/PC Amber is best for dark sky. Metal halide and cool LEDs are worst. Hover ? for ranked guidance.
    CCT (K)Correlated Colour Temperature in Kelvin. ≤2200K = best practice (amber). ≤2700K = acceptable. 3000K+ = not recommended. 4000K+ = harmful blue-rich light. Check the lamp or fixture label, or measure with a spectrometer.
    WattsEnergy input in watts. Found on the lamp or driver label. Used to calculate total site energy consumption.
    LumensLight output in lumens. Found on the lamp label. Optional but useful for calculating illuminance levels.
    Control & compliance
    When neededWhen the light is actually required. "All night" is rarely justified — most sites can use dusk-to-curfew or motion-activated schedules.
    Control typeHow the light is switched. Motion sensor and photosensor are preferred for dark sky. Manual switch with no timer is least preferred.
    Operable?Is the fixture currently working? Record "Broken – TBD" for fixtures that need attention — they should be flagged for follow-up.
    Compliant?Does the fixture meet all five RASC principles? Yes / No / To be determined. Use TBD for fixtures where you need more information before deciding.

    Spectrum data

    A spectrum screenshot shows the spectral power distribution (SPD) of the light source — essentially how much energy the lamp emits at each wavelength. Blue-rich light (peaks around 450–480 nm) is most harmful to dark skies and wildlife. Amber and warm-white sources have minimal blue content.

    How to capture a spectrum
    1
    Download a spectrometer app on your phone (see list below).
    2
    Point the phone camera at the luminaire from a safe distance.
    3
    Take a screenshot of the spectrum display showing the full SPD curve.
    4
    Upload the screenshot to the fixture's Spectrum screenshot slot in the Photos & Spectra tab.
    Recommended apps
    Spectroid (Android, free) — real-time audio/light spectrogram. Best free option.
    Spectrometer+ (iOS, paid) — camera-based light spectrum with CCT reading.
    USB spectrometer (e.g. Ocean Optics, Hamamatsu) — export SPD chart as PNG/JPG.
    Any CCT/CRI meter screenshot — if you have a calibrated light meter that shows colour data, a screenshot of its display is useful.
    Data & backup
    Where is my data stored?
    All data — site info, fixture records, photos, and spectra — is stored in your browser's local storage. It stays on your device and is not sent anywhere. Different browsers on the same computer have separate storage.
    How do I back up my data?
    Go to Report & Map, select your site, and click CSV. Save the file somewhere safe — a shared drive, email to yourself, or cloud storage. The CSV contains everything including photos (as base64 image data).
    How do I restore my data on a new device or browser?
    Go to the Import tab, click Import CSV, and upload your saved CSV file. The site and all fixtures will be recreated automatically including photos and spectra.
    How large can my data get?
    Browsers allow roughly 5–10 MB of local storage. Photos are resized to max 1200px (~100–200 KB each) on upload, so you can store around 30–50 photos before approaching limits. Export CSV regularly to stay safe.
    Can I share data with my team?
    Export CSV and send it to a colleague. They import it on the Import tab. Currently the tool is single-user per browser — a hosted multi-user version is on the roadmap. See the GitHub repository for details.
    Can I use the CSV in Excel?
    Yes — open Excel, go to Data → From Text/CSV, and import the file. The photo columns contain long base64 strings which Excel will show but won't render as images. For viewing photos, use the tool's lightbox view.
    Troubleshooting
    The page looks outdated or tabs don't work after an update
    Your browser has cached an old version. Press Ctrl+Shift+R (Windows/Linux) or Cmd+Shift+R (Mac) for a hard refresh. On Opera use Ctrl+F5. If that doesn't work, clear your browser cache via Settings → Privacy → Clear browsing data → Cached images and files.
    GPS coordinates are not being extracted from my photos
    Check that location access is enabled for your camera app (see GPS Setup). Screenshots never contain GPS — you must use the Camera app directly. When transferring photos, use original quality — compressed/resized versions strip EXIF data. Some third-party camera apps don't embed GPS even with permissions granted; use the default iOS or Android Camera app.
    My data disappeared after clearing my browser
    Clearing browser data removes local storage. This is why regular CSV exports are important. If you have a recent CSV export, import it on the Import tab to restore your data. Going forward, export CSV after every session.
    Photos aren't saving or the tool runs slowly with many photos
    Photos are resized to max 1200px on upload, but if you have many fixtures with multiple photos, local storage can fill up. Export CSV to back up and free up storage. If the tool is unresponsive, try a different browser — Chrome and Edge generally handle large local storage better than Firefox or Safari.
    CSV import says "No rows to import" or nothing happens
    Make sure you click Import CSV first to show the upload zone, then upload the file. The tool expects a CSV exported from this tool (with "Site Name" as the first column) or an older RASC format. Excel files (.xlsx) are not supported — save as CSV first in Excel via File → Save As → CSV UTF-8.
    The fixture map shows no pins
    The map only shows fixtures that have GPS coordinates. Upload a geo-tagged photo (with GPS enabled on your camera) or use the "Capture GPS now" button inside the fixture edit form. The GPS button requires browser location permission — allow it when prompted.
